William Clark

Written By Kaiden Lund

The famous explorer William Clark was born on August 1, 1770, in Caroline County, Virginia. He was the ninth child of ten his parents were native of King and Queen County. They were english and maybe scottish. They were planters in virginia they owned several estates they owned several slaves. They went to anglican church. Clarks five older brothers were fighting in virginia during the American Revolution. William was to young to fight his oldest brother was jonathan clark and he also served as a colonel during the war he did well and eventually was ranked to brigadier general in the virginia militia. The second oldest George Rogers Clark Got the rank of General he only really fought in kentucky. The clarks and their slaves went to kentucky they first went to redstone landing in present down Brownsville, Pennsylvania


Lewis recruited clark then agreed to share command of the new corps of discovery. Their mission was to explore the territory of the louisiana Purchase establish trade with other natives. They also were to find a stream or river from the US to the pacific and claim oregon for the US before other european areas claimed them. Clarks also spent 3 years on the expedition to the coast of the pacific. There also was a slave owner known to deal not so great with his slaves he broung york one of his many slaves with him the Indigenous nations treated york nicely and respectfully. The natives were also fascinated by his appearance. Clark refused a promotion to captain from jefferson also asked the senate to appoint him. Clark concentrated on drawing the maps.
